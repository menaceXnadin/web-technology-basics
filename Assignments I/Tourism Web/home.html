About the City

Kathmandu, Nepal's capital, is set in a valley surrounded by the Himalayan mountains. At the heart of the old cityâ€™s mazelike alleys is Durbar Square, which becomes frenetic during Indra Jatra, a religious festival featuring masked dances. Many of the city's historic sites were damaged or destroyed by a 2015 earthquake. Durbar Square's palace, Hanuman Dhoka, and Kasthamandap, a wooden Hindu temple, are being rebuilt. Nestled within the majestic Himalayas, Kathmandu, Nepal's capital, beckons travelers with its captivating blend of ancient culture, spiritual sanctuaries, and breathtaking landscapes . Steeped in history, the city's UNESCO World Heritage sites like Durbar Square and Swayambhunath Stupa stand as testaments to its rich architectural heritage, while the spiritual aura of Pashupatinath Temple and Boudhanath Stupa offers tranquil moments for introspection. Beyond the historical treasures, Kathmandu pulsates with vibrant energy, especially in Thamel, where bustling streets, local markets, and diverse cuisine paint a vivid picture of daily life. Moreover, the city serves as a gateway to Himalayan adventures, offering trekking opportunities and breathtaking panoramas that attract outdoor enthusiasts worldwide. Kathmandu, with its cultural depth and natural beauty, invites visitors to explore, discover, and immerse themselves in the soul-stirring experiences it generously offers.
