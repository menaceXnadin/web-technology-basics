Basantapur

HISTORY

Basantapur, also known as Kathmandu Durbar Square, is a historic square located in the heart of Kathmandu. It is an ancient palace complex that once served as the royal residence of the Malla kings and showcases exquisite Nepalese architecture. The construction of this architectural marvel dates back to the 16th century, and it stands as a testament to Nepal's rich cultural heritage.

Description

Basantapur Square is a vibrant hub featuring palaces, courtyards, temples, and various other historical monuments. The square is surrounded by intricately carved wooden structures, pagoda-style temples, and the majestic Hanuman Dhoka Palace. Visitors can immerse themselves in the cultural tapestry by exploring museums, art galleries, and observing daily rituals and festivals that take place within this architectural gem. Basantapur Square captivates with its blend of history, art, and religious significance, attracting both locals and tourists alike.
