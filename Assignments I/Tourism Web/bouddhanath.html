Boudhanath

HISTORY

Boudhanath, also known as Boudha, is one of the largest and most important Buddhist stupas in Nepal. It is situated in the eastern outskirts of Kathmandu and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The stupa's construction dates back to the 5th century, and it is believed to enshrine the remains of Kassapa Buddha, making it a significant pilgrimage site for followers of Buddhism.

Description

Boudhanath is characterized by its massive mandala, a representation of the Buddhist universe, adorned with colorful prayer flags and prayer wheels. The stupa's white dome is topped with a gilded spire and encompassed by a circular market bustling with shops, monasteries, and cafes. Pilgrims and visitors can be seen circumambulating the stupa, spinning prayer wheels and chanting mantras as part of their devotional practices. The area exudes a tranquil ambiance, amplified by the mesmerizing sight of monks, prayer flags fluttering in the breeze, and the scent of incense permeating the air.
