Budhanilkantha

HISTORY

Budhanilkantha, or the "Sleeping Vishnu," is an iconic representation of Lord Vishnu lying on a coiled serpent, Ananta Shesha. This ancient stone carving dates back to the Lichhavi period and holds immense religious significance for Hindus in Nepal.

Description

Budhanilkantha is located in the northern outskirts of Kathmandu and draws devotees and tourists alike. The impressive stone sculpture depicts Lord Vishnu in a reclining position on the cosmic ocean, symbolizing his cosmic dream that maintains the balance of the universe. The serene atmosphere around the deity is enhanced by the surrounding pond and the peaceful environment of the temple complex, making it a significant religious and cultural site in Nepal.
